1. Seasonal Temperature Profiles

The depth and properties of the mixed layer of the ocean does not stay the same throughout the year. The following image shows the development of a temperature profile between summer and winter for a science station in the North East Pacific Ocean (Station OWS Papa).

knitr::include_graphics("annual_cycle.jpg")

  1. Write down the depth and temperature of the mixed layer for each profile shown. How would you simply describe to trends from August to April in these values?

Two major factors influence the changes in the depth and temperature of the mixed layer:

  • A colder atmosphere will cool the surface layer. A warmer atmosphere will warm the surface layer.
  • Stronger winds will drive more stirring in the upper water. Less wind will tend to allow waters to become more stratified.

As we move from the summer to the winter, the winds get progressively stronger and colder.

  1. Can you use this information to describe and explain the evolution of the mixed layer from Summer to Winter?

Below 120 m there is little change in temperature between seasons.

  1. Why is this?

  2. How do you expect the evolution of the mixed layer to impact other oceanographic properties such as surface nutrient availability and oxygen content?

3. More on the Carbon Pump

The oceanic carbon pumps are not quite as simple as we’ve been discussing so far. Along with the standard biological pump where detritus falls to the depths by gravity, there are a few nuanced additions to the way the ocean moves carbon from the surface to the deep ocean.

This is nicely summarized in the following article:

Multi-faceted particle pumps drive carbon sequestration in the ocean by Philip W. Boyd, Hervé Claustre, Marina Levy, David A. Siegel & Thomas Weber

Read the paper above and think about the following questions:

  1. What tools have scientists traditionally used to measure the biological pump?

  2. What additional pumps exist in the ocean?

  3. How important a role might they play in the overall amount of carbon that reaches the deep ocean?
