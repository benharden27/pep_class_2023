We crowd sourced a good amount of this level content as a class including setting up some basic criteria for the creation of a successful team.

What came to the surface through this exercise is that a team is successful if they can work together effectively to reach a common goal. This goal is typically not achievable by any one individual alone.

Some features of successful teams that we discussed:

  • Strong communication strategies including clear expectations, etiquette, and ability to resolve conflicts constructively

  • Distinct roles such that everyone knows what part they play and can take ownership and have buy-in.

  • Equitable division of labor: Fair is not always equal, but everyone should feel they are contributing what they can and taking an equitable burden.

  • A clear plan. Knowing your goal is one this, but success in reaching it usually requires a clear plan and a system to both enact the plan and make decisions should the plan not work as effectively as they had hoped.
